chapters:
  - num: "I. The Brief"
    lead: Not another café.
    body: |
      The operator had already owned two coffee businesses in Dhaka. Both had failed gracefully, for the same reason. They were cafés. Nothing more. The city is full of cafés and the marginal one does not last.

      The brief we agreed on, across a long afternoon and a notebook, was that the third attempt would not be a café that also played music. It would be a room organised around listening, which happened to serve coffee. The distinction looks small on paper. It decides everything that follows.
  - num: "II. The Name"
    lead: A house, not a shop.
    body: |
      **House** is the jazz word. A house band. A house orchestra. A house set. It is also the Dhaka domestic register: the door a neighbour walks through without knocking. **Café** is the category, listed plainly, in the same weight as the rest of the name, so the category cannot hide.

      We resisted a more clever name, and a more poetic one, and a Bangla-first one. The most useful thing the name could do was get out of the way of the room.
  - num: "III. The Menu"
    lead: A side A and a side B.
    body: |
      The menu is structured as an LP. *Side A* is morning and coffee. *Side B* is afternoon and kitchen. There are two specials a week, written in by hand, and a footnote rather than a dessert list. The menu is one folded sheet of Hahnemühle, reprinted weekly, numbered in the corner like a pressing.

      Every dish is named for a recording. The Monk, the Ella, the Ravi. Guests who know the reference know it. Guests who don't read an ordinary menu and order an ordinary breakfast.
    pullquote: The menu is one folded sheet, reprinted weekly, numbered in the corner like a pressing.
  - num: "IV. Music Direction"
    lead: Jazz-era Dhaka.
    body: |
      Most of the difficult thinking went into the music. The city had a brief, astonishing jazz scene in the late 1960s: radio broadcasts out of what was then called Dhaka Radio, visiting orchestras, a small club circuit that has since been paved over. We commissioned a researcher to sit in the national archive for six weeks and return with every recording, photograph, and programme note from 1965 to 1971 she could legitimately copy.

      What came back became the basis for the house programme. Monday is Blue Note. Tuesday is the archive. Wednesday is new pressings. Thursday is listening night, a single record played end to end, lights low, no conversation louder than the speaker.
  - num: "V. Notes in Closing"
    lead: What the work is for.
    body: |
      House Café has been in service since the autumn of 2024. Thursday listening night is booked two weeks out. The third attempt has not failed.

      The conviction was that a room, disciplined, can ask something of the people in it, and they will meet the ask. This remains the conviction.
