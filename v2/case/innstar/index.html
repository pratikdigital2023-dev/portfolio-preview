InnStar Brand System.

Six practices under one holding company, shaped over ten years into a single editorial logic. The argument that a luxury ensemble could be read, from the outside, as one clear voice in six registers.

I — The premise

One voice, six registers.

In 2016, InnStar owned three operating practices and two more were drafted on a whiteboard. Each had its own logo, colour, brochure, and tone. Read together they read as five companies, not one.

The argument, made first on paper and then slowly made in the work, was that a holding company is not the sum of its practices. It is a position.

II — Architecture

Branded house, quietly.

The architecture arrived at is a branded-house system. InnStar is the mark of origin. Each practice carries a practice-level mark, set in the same typographic grammar, in the same weight and width, with a single-letter prefix.

“The recognition is carried by the system, not by the logo.”
III — Tone of voice

Restraint as a discipline.

The tone guide is four pages. It fits on four pages because most of it is a list of what we do not do. We do not use the words solutions, premium, exclusive, world-class, passionate, unique, journey, partnership, or excellence.

The positive guidance is shorter still. Write short sentences. Name what is named. Decline the sales line. Trust the reader.

IV — Record

Ten years of the same conviction.

Signage and wayfinding across 19 residential and commercial projects. Three properties to LEED certification. Campaigns run in partnership with Telenor Group and Meta. Market entry for Jinpeng EV in 2025.

None of this was the position. The position was the discipline that produced it, and the discipline that will produce whatever is next.

V — Notes in closing

A long argument.

A brand system is not a guidelines document. It is a long argument, made over years, that a business can behave with editorial consistency while its practices continue to differ in the world.

The underlying conviction remains intact. The luxury register will hold if it is asked to.
