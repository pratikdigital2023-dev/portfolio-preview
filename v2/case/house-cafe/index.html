II Operation Two · the prime factor

House Café.

A neighbourhood café reimagined as a small listening room. Identity, menu architecture, interior direction, and a music programme drawn from the jazz-era city that Dhaka once was.

I — The brief

Not another café.

The operator had already owned two coffee businesses in Dhaka. Both had failed gracefully, for the same reason. They were cafés. Nothing more.

The brief we agreed on, across a long afternoon and a notebook, was that the third attempt would not be a café that also played music. It would be a room organised around listening, which happened to serve coffee.

II — The menu

A side A and a side B.

The menu is structured as an LP. Side A is morning and coffee. Side B is afternoon and kitchen. There are two specials a week, written in by hand, and a footnote rather than a dessert list. The menu is one folded sheet, reprinted weekly, numbered in the corner like a pressing.

One folded sheet, reprinted weekly, numbered in the corner like a pressing.
III — Music direction

Jazz-era Dhaka.

The city had a brief, astonishing jazz scene in the late 1960s. We commissioned a researcher to sit in the national archive for six weeks and return with every recording, photograph, and programme note from 1965 to 1971 she could legitimately copy.

Monday is Blue Note. Tuesday is the archive. Wednesday is new pressings. Thursday is listening night, a single record played end to end, lights low, no conversation louder than the speaker.

IV — Notes in closing

A room, disciplined.

House Café has been in service since the autumn of 2024. Thursday listening night is booked two weeks out. The third attempt has not failed.

The conviction was that a room, disciplined, can ask something of the people in it, and they will meet the ask.
